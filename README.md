# 🎩 Hatcraft

*Wouldn't you wanna put a banner on your head and feel like a captain? Or wear an end rod and look like a unicorn? Perhaps a cactus flower to look like a princess? Or a glass block as an autronaut helmet? Maybe a cute pet copper golem chilling on your head?*

This simple data pack allows players to turn any item and block in the game into a hat, equippable in the head armor slot. The goal is to provide players with fun and silly cosmetics, to be used for character decoration, lore, role-playing, etc.

## 👓 How to use

There are two ways to turn an item into a hat:

- Simple-hat: Drop items onto a loom, and they will become equippable. These hats have no armor points, durability, nor enchantments.
- Helmet-hat: Drop a single item onto a smithing table and hold a helmet in your off-hand. The helmet will be consumed and all of its components (such as durability, enchantments, custom name, etc.) will be transfered to your new hat.

## 📑 Notes

- Theoretically, any item and block can be converted into a hat, even non-vanilla ones, as long as they allow using vanilla components to store equippiblity, durability, and so on. However, only vanilla helmets can be used to craft helmet-hats.
- The hats are still items/blocks. So, if you accidentally use one in a crafting recipe or place it down, it will lose all of its components and become an ordinary item/block. For this reason, a warning will appear in the action bar while you are holding a helmet-hat.
- Helmet-hats can not be enchanted once created. So, be sure to apply all of your desired enchantments on your helmet before consuming it. They can be repaired via anvil and mending however.
- You can apply another helmet on an already existing helmet-hat by dropping it on a smithing table again. The new helmet's components will override the old one's.
